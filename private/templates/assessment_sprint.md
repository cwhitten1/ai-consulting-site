# Assessment/Sprint Template

**Purpose**: Use this to scope and run a 3-5 day AI Opportunity Assessment. Covers everything from SOW through delivery.  
**Audience**: You (the consultant). Internal operational guide.

---

## When to Use

After a qualified discovery call (see `discovery_call.md`). The prospect has a specific pain point, data exists, and budget authority is confirmed.

---

## Scope Definition

### Include (always)
- [ ] Stakeholder interviews (2-4 people max)
- [ ] Current workflow mapping for target process(es)
- [ ] Technology stack and data readiness review
- [ ] AI opportunity identification and scoring
- [ ] Prioritized roadmap with recommended pilot
- [ ] Executive summary presentation (30 min)

### Explicitly Out of Scope
- [ ] Building any software or prototypes
- [ ] Accessing production data or systems
- [ ] Vendor selection or procurement
- [ ] Implementation of recommendations
- [ ] Ongoing support post-delivery

*If they ask for something out of scope during the sprint, say: "Great idea — I'll add it to the roadmap as a potential pilot."*

---

## Day-by-Day Methodology

| Day | Focus | Activities | Output |
|-----|-------|------------|--------|
| 1 | Discovery | Stakeholder interviews (2-4 × 45 min), collect existing docs, map org structure | Interview notes, stakeholder map |
| 2 | Analysis | Map target workflows end-to-end, identify data sources, assess AI readiness | Process maps, data inventory |
| 3 | Opportunities | Score opportunities (Impact × Feasibility ÷ Risk), identify quick wins vs. strategic bets | Opportunity matrix |
| 4 | Synthesis | Draft findings document, build prioritized roadmap, design recommended pilot | Draft report |
| 5 | Delivery | Present findings to stakeholders (30 min), handle Q&A, deliver final document | Final report + presentation |

**3-day variant**: Compress Days 2-3 into one day. Works for smaller companies (<30 people) with fewer processes.

---

## Deliverables Checklist

Your final delivery package:

- [ ] **Current State Map** — How the target process works today (diagram or structured description)
- [ ] **Opportunity Matrix** — Scored list of AI opportunities (Impact, Feasibility, Risk, Timeline)
- [ ] **Prioritized Roadmap** — Recommended sequence: quick wins (0-30 days), medium-term (1-3 months), strategic (3-6 months)
- [ ] **Recommended Pilot** — Specific project with scope, expected outcome, success criteria, rough timeline
- [ ] **Executive Summary** — 1-2 page standalone summary for decision-makers who won't read the full report

|-------------|---|---|---|---|---|
| _[fill in]_ | _ | _ | _ | Y/N | Impact × Feasibility ÷ Risk |

**Feasibility**: Technical simplicity, data availability, integration complexity

## During the Sprint: Guardrails

- **Don't sell during the assessment** — You're here to understand, not pitch
- **Don't promise outcomes you haven't validated** — "This looks promising" ≠ "This will work"
- **Scope creep response**: "That's a great question — I'll add it to the roadmap"
- **If you find nothing worth pursuing**: Say so honestly. Recommend they wait or focus elsewhere. Protect the referral relationship.
- **Daily check-in**: 10-min standup with client POC — what you did today, what you need tomorrow

## Handoff

On completion, if pilot recommended → proceed to **poc_pilot.md** (start at "Pilot Scope Definition") to scope and run the proof-of-concept. Your Assessment's "Recommended Pilot" deliverable becomes the pilot's scope input.

If no pilot recommended → deliver findings, suggest revisiting in 3-6 months, maintain relationship.
