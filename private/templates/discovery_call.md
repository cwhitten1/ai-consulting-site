# Discovery Call Template

**Purpose**: Use this before every first call with a prospect. Guides a 30-min conversation from rapport through qualification to clear next steps.  
**Audience**: You (the consultant). This is your internal runbook, not a client-facing doc.

---

## Pre-Call Prep (15 min before)

- [ ] Read their website — what they sell, who they serve
- [ ] Check LinkedIn for attendee roles
- [ ] Identify likely workflows (document-heavy, repetitive, high-volume)
- [ ] Note industry-specific AI opportunities (hold loosely — don't sell these)
- [ ] Confirm: Who referred them? What's the relationship to protect?

---

## Meeting Structure (30 min)

| Time | Phase | Goal |
|------|-------|------|
| 0-3 min | Intro | Rapport, set agenda ("I'll ask questions, then share initial thoughts") |
| 3-15 min | Pain Points | Understand their world — current state, frustrations, goals |
| 15-22 min | Qualification | Assess fit — budget signals, timeline, decision authority |
| 22-28 min | Initial Value | Share 1-2 observations, demonstrate you understand their problem |
| 28-30 min | Next Steps | Propose specific action (assessment, follow-up, or graceful decline) |

---

## Question Bank

### Current State
- What does your company do? How many people?
- What's your existing software stack? (Microsoft 365? Google? CRM? Databases?)
- Have you experimented with AI? What worked? What didn't?

### Desired State
- If we met again in 90 days, what would make this a success?
- What's the #1 bottleneck frustrating your team right now?
- What work is repetitive that skilled people shouldn't be doing?

### Constraints & AI Readiness
- Security requirements? Compliance? Data restrictions?
- What approval processes exist for new tools/spend?
- Any past vendor experiences (good or bad) I should know about?
- Where does your data live? (Structured DB, shared drives, email, paper?)
- Who owns the data? Any sensitivity concerns (PII, HIPAA, classified)?

### Timeline & Budget Signals
- Is there a timeline driving this? (Contract deadline, hiring freeze, seasonal push?)
- Have you budgeted for AI/automation work, or is this exploratory?
- Who else would need to approve moving forward?

---

## Qualification Flags

### Green Flags (good fit)
- [ ] Specific, articulable pain point (not "we want AI")
- [ ] Data exists and is accessible (even if messy)
- [ ] Budget allocated or clear authority to allocate
- [ ] Executive sponsor identified (person who can say yes)
- [ ] Timeline is realistic (not "we need this tomorrow")
- [ ] Referral relationship is strong

### Red Flags (pause or decline)
- [ ] No clear problem — "we just want to explore AI"
- [ ] No executive sponsor — talking to someone who can't approve
- [ ] Sensitive data with no governance (HIPAA, PII, classified without clearance)
- [ ] Unrealistic expectations ("full automation in 2 weeks")
- [ ] Shopping for the cheapest vendor (price-only decision)
- [ ] Previous AI failures with no willingness to do things differently

---

## Decision Tree: What to Propose

```
Green flags ≥ 4, Red flags = 0
  → Propose AI Opportunity Assessment (→ assessment_sprint.md)
  → Send follow-up email within 24hr with 1-page summary + assessment proposal

Green flags ≥ 2, Red flags ≤ 1
  → Send free 1-2 page findings summary
  → Offer to reconnect in 2-4 weeks
  → "What is the smallest engagement I can confidently deliver that creates obvious value?"

Red flags ≥ 2 OR Green flags < 2
  → Graceful decline: "I don't think I'm the right fit for where you are right now"
  → Offer: a resource, a referral, or "let's reconnect when X changes"
  → Protect the referral relationship above all
```

---

## Post-Call Actions (within 24 hours)

- [ ] Send thank-you email with 2-3 bullet summary of what you heard
- [ ] If qualified: attach 1-page "What I Learned" summary (current state, opportunities, recommended next step, rough ROI)
- [ ] If proposing assessment: include scope overview + fixed fee (see `assessment_sprint.md`)
- [ ] Update your pipeline tracker
- [ ] Thank the referrer (if applicable)
